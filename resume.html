N.C College of Engineering, Israna, Panipat

  • Date of Viva-Voce/ Defense: 17th September 2016
    Date of Award of Degree: 14th October 2016
    Ph.D Course Work Pass Percentage: 76.11%
    Thesis Title : A Pragmatic Approach to COTS Selection in Designing Component Based Software System.

  • The work was to apply techniques of Software Engineering to modeling and optimization for designing a Component Based Software System. The objectives of our work can be divided into four stages.

    1. The first stage identifies and classifies factors for evaluating the success of software developed by Commercial of The Shelf (COTS) components.

    2. Every business has got some parameters which are essential for its success and they are known as Critical Success Factors (CSFs). In the second stage, we have tried to identify and categorize the CSF for software developed using COTS components. For this, a framework known as TOPVEC (Technical, Organizational, People, Vendor Selection, and Cost) has been proposed in this research.

    3. The third stage focuses on COTS vendor selection- A Multi-criteria intuitionistic fuzzy set approach with TOPSIS (technique for order preference by similarity to an ideal solution) method has been used to evaluate and rank the COTS vendors. The criteria for evaluation of COTS vendor has been taken from stage two.

    4. An optimization model for component selection has been formulated in stage four with vendor ranking as objective function, that has been derived from stage three. The optimization model uses build or buy strategy for component selection.
